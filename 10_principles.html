Dieter Rams: 10 Principles for Good Design


1. Good design is innovative
Without innovative design, we would see the same things over and over again. While I don't think that designers need to contantly be reinventing the wheel, it is important to keep up with innovation in technology.

2. Good design makes a product useful
I agree that when designing a product one of the main goals should be its usefulness. When designing I keep the viewer/user in mind to remember how this will be useful to them.

3. Good design is aesthetic
While the functionality of a product is most important, it takes a pleasing aesthetic for a user to desire it compared to a less pretty design. I try to at least execute my designs well; whether someone is a fan of the style or not is up to them.

4. Good design makes a product understandable
I agree that designs that are easy to understand are some of the best. I would rather have a design be oversimplified than overcomplicated.

5. Good design is unobtrusive
I agree that the design of a product should be unobtrusive to its purpose, but I also think that it's nice to have products that are pleasing to look at even if they aren't meant to be works of art.


6. Good design is honest
I agree that a well-designed product should not trick the consumer into thinking that can do any more than it actually can, or is better than it actually is.

7. Good design is long-lasting
It is important for a design to be long-lasting so that no matter how much time has passed it won't seem outdated.

8. Good design is thorough down to the last detail
Mistakes happen but I agree that design should come from well-thought-out decisions.

9. Good design is environmentally-friendly
Being environmentally-friendly is definitely something I try to keep in mind when designing. Wasting paper is something that I actively have to try to avoid.

10. Good design is as little design as possible
I agree because often times putting too much thought into a design can ruin it by adding unnecessary things. When I design I try to keep things as simple as possible.
